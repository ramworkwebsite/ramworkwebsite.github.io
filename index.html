Shipping container residential addition

This beautiful blue structure acts as an extension to the original building, and is elevated 1 storey (via moment frame) to provide parking below. The shipping container rooftop privacy screen was engineered to be safe yet easy on the eyes. My involvement was the complete structural design and inspection while at Contact Engineering. (Arch. and Constr. by others)

Custom 3 storey residential

How do you blow out the back walls of a house for elegant glass doors and windows without comprimising structural integrity? The answer: multi storey moment frames. My involvement was the complete structural design and inspection while at Contact Engineering. (Arch. and Constr. by others)

Skytable flying shoring platform

Is it a bird? Is it a plane? No, this is PERI's skytable flying slab-shoring system. Used often in multi-storey buildings, the platform is able to be crane-lifted between levels to save time and labour when forming concrete slabs. My involvement was the complete structural design and inspection while at Peri Canada. (Construction by others)

Custom 2 storey residential

This house made use of underpinning to create a deep and spacious basement, as well as LVL beams to vault a towering roof profile. Infilled with a floor addition, mezzanine, bay windows cantilevers, and carport slab, this expansive renovation is quite a remarkable feat of residential engineering. My involvement was the complete structural design and inspection while at Contact Engineering. (Arch. and Constr. by others)

GTA Condo

Early on in my career at Peri Canada, this massive project was my pride and joy. The project included a midrise concrete condo and 3 adjacent townhouses for which I had designed all of the slab shoring and wall formwork. As you can imagine, this project's drawing set for temp. works was extensive, detailed, and emphasized cost efficient design. (Structural EOR, Arch. and Constr. by others)

Engineered wood specialty

A good engineer is one that has extensive knowledge of their building materials and what to use when the first material does not work out. This photo illustrates using full height LSL studs in the design instead of wood studs when typical wood studs are too short to span multistorey. My involvement was the structural design of the foundations and inspection while at Contact Engineering. (Arch. and Constr. by others)

Maximo 6m tall formed concrete walls

6ft is pretty tall when it comes to the average human. 6m is hard to imagine until you are on site next to a 6m form for a poured concrete wall. My involvement was the design of temporary forms and bracing while at Peri Canada. (Structural EOR, Arch. and Constr. by others).

Downtown 3 storey triplex

One of my first projects at Contact Engineering was this 3 storey triplex. The structure is mainly wood framed, but also utilizes cold formed steel design for non-combustible construction requirements. This project first introduced me to cold formed steel design in combination with wood and structural steel. My involvement was the structural design of the foundations and inspection while at Contact Engineering. (Arch. and Constr. by others)

Demolition Permit Temporary Works

When a house is built 100 years ago and starts slanting like a sinking ship, how do you safely demolish it without blowing a hole in your wallet? This was a great project where I used wood to temporarily shore and brace an entire 2-storey house from the inside while it was being demolished. My involvement was the design of the temporary works while at Contact Engineering. (Constr. by others)

Modular housing
